How a score is built

Nothing here is a black box. Open any score in the app and it shows every ability, every metric inside it, and what each one counted for.

01

Rank inside the pool

Every metric becomes a percentile against players in the same position, league and season. Volume alone never scores.

02

Group into abilities

Percentiles gather into the six or seven things a position is actually judged on. An ability with too little behind it is left blank rather than guessed.

03

Weight what matters

Scoring counts more for a striker than aerial duels do. The weights are published, not hidden, and they differ by position.

04

Say how firm it is

A score from a pool of sixteen is coarser than one from a hundred. Where it is coarse, the app says so instead of pretending to a decimal it has not earned.

Questions

Where does the data come from?

Match and season statistics are collected from a licensed sports data feed, then processed here into percentiles, abilities and scores. Nothing is scraped and nothing is invented.

Why is a Championship 99 not the same as a La Liga 99?

Because every pool has a best player. A score says how a player ranks among his own peers, so the top of any league reaches the top of its own scale. The adjusted column exists for exactly this reason: it shifts each figure by how strong its league is, so the two can be read side by side.

Can I trust the defensive scores?

Partly, and the app is honest about which part. Defending is measured by counting actions, and a defender at a dominant side defends less often. Checked against how much a team keeps the ball, the final score does not reward defenders whose teams are overrun — but positioning and pressing go unmeasured, and no score can see what they prevent.

How often is it updated?

Weekly during the season, as new fixtures land in the feed. Each score carries the date its dataset was built.
